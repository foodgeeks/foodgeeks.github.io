6 ways we made the Tummy food journal faster and more intuitive

← Back — Posted May 2018 — by Ryan Snyder

Food logging is clunky. It takes too much time. It’s unintuitive. You hate it. And so do we. Well, we used to. ;)

If you or your client are working through food-triggered symptoms or other dietary issues, a food journal is almost necessary to help you identify the foods that aren’t working for your body. So we put a significant amount of effort into making the Tummy food journal a faster, more intuitive experience.

1. Enter Meals on the Meal Page

You no longer have to enter foods on a separate page — you now enter your meal items within the meal page.

2. Searching is no longer required

Just type in your food, and we make the best guess for the food we think you consumed. If it’s the wrong match, quickly find a better match, and Tummy will remember the match the next time you enter that food.

My own personal breakfast of champions

3. We love frequent foods

Your meal items for a particular meal are automatically saved into a frequent foods list. Tap the heart, and add your typical breakfast in under 15 seconds every morning.

Adding frequent meal items

4. Instant meal stats

We added meal stats, so you can keep better tabs on the “Big 6”, as we call them. Monitor progress towards meeting your daily fruit, vegetable, and fiber requirements, and make sure you don’t go overboard on sugar, caffeine, and alcohol. If you’re a nutritionist, you can still remove calories and macronutrients from your clients’ meals so that your clients can focus on intuitive eating.

Nutritional stats available for each meal

Nutritional stats available for each day

5. Add a photo of your meal

Personally, there have been a number of times where I’ve tried to enter a meal at the end of a busy day, and I couldn’t remember what I ate for lunch. Now, I snap a photo of my lunch in the Tummy app, and at the end of the day I’ll have a visual cue that helps me properly log my meal.

Biscuits -n- Gravy — clean eating at its finest :)

6. British food products

Finally, we added support for British food products by integrating with Tesco’s database of foods. If you can find a food at Tesco, then you’ll now find it on Tummy. So brew up some PG Tips, and dunk a few McVitie’s rich tea biscuits in your cuppa, and we’ll have you covered in the food journal!

Stay tuned, as we’ll add barcode scanning into Tummy in the next release.

Tummy allows a nutritionists’ clients to log their meals, symptoms, and body measurements, and helps nutritionists keep up with their clients in between sessions. Find out more at https://www.tummy.io
